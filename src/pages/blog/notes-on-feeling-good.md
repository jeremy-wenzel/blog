Here is a list of things that help me feel good through out the day

## Waking up

Waking up has always been difficult for me. Many a time, the night before I am full of excitement of how the time I had available by waking up early. But then I wake up feeling groggy and unable to actually do anything. Or even worse, I would just go back to sleep, wake up later than I wanted to, and feel awful that I didn't meet my expectations of getting up early or doing the things I "wanted" to do. 

1. Get at least 7 hours of sleep. My body really wants 8 and I strive to achieve that whenever possible but it isn't always feasible with my busy schedule
2. Take the dog for at least a 30 minute walk. About 15 minutes into the walk I start feeling more normal and alert so the extra 15 mintues help ensure I feel a lot better
3. Drink 3 glasses of water. I feel a lot of times I am still feeling groggy or fog-headed is because I am dehydrated so go overboard to make sure my body has enough water has helped me get my head feeling good
4. Stretching hip flexors and strengthen glues: I take about 10-15 minutes to do some stretching of the hip flexors and hip thrusts to strengthen my glutes. I sit a lot, even with a standing desk availble to me, so stretching the things that get tight and strengthen the things that get loose prevents those areas getting achy, preventing me from focusing.
5. Take a cold shower. I frequently sweat, at least a little bit, during the night. If it is warm while taking the dog for a walk, I will also get a little bit of a sweat. Taking a cold "rinse off" shower has made me just feel like a normal human
6. Eye drops and allergy meds. My eyes are super dry for whatever reason, so some eyedrops plus allergy meds make feel like normal
7. Eating a couple hours after I wake up. I find eating right after I get up makes me feel tired and lethargic just a little while after.

I am not really someone who believes in a "morning routine" mental masturbation is something that everyone needs. However, I do believe that people need to listen to their body and what they need. I realized how I felt, which was fog headed and groggy, and started experiementing with things that will help me feel like a human after I wake up. These things work for me and might not work for someone else. But I hope this helps you try to figure out how to listen to what your body is telling you so that you give it what it needs to get up and productive!

## Body

As a programmer, I am pretty sedentary. I sit. And sit. Then sit some more. I occasionally use a standing desk thorughout the day but after about 30 minutes, I am wanting to sit again.

This leads to some issues that can get so annoying that I can't focus
* Glutes feel extremely achy and Hip flexors feel super tight. Basically, my whole hip just feels awful to the point I can't focus.
* Shoulder blades feel achy and even massaging them doesn't help

To solve, I do the following
* The hip issue is two fold. Sitting is causing the hip flexors to tighten up and the glutes to loosen because they are being stretched. So the solution is to stretch the hip flex by getting on one knee and leaning foward. Do this on both sides for 60-90 seconds for a couple sets really helps losen it up.
* The next is the glutes which is using hip thrusts. Sit on the floor and do some hip thrusts, holding for like 3 seconds and doing 20 of them.
* For the shoulders, this requires some working out. Doing a workout that targets the delts, rotator cuff, and the lower traps helps keep things in place and reduces the ache.
